About the Network

Our network was started to be a solution to what our community felt were problems for the anonymous community in general on other networks.

For example, Freenode.net has a great community full of open source projects and technically skilled individuals. But there is no anonymous registration. This puts people in oppressed nations without freedom of speech in danger.

Many Anonymous networks who did allow anonymous registration were hostile enviroments with little empathy from the network operators.

Our network has the following definite purposes:
1) Provide a Network promoting Privacy, Anonymity and Freedom.
2) Provide an network friendly to the culture of Anonymous, the concept of freedom.
This allows people from all over the world to speak on matters they feel are important, anonymously.

Although hackers are welcome to our network, you do not need to be a hacker to be welcome. Our goal is to promote our philosophy, not teach everyone computer science.



FAQ

1) Isn't Anonymous a "Hacktivist" Group?
A) No. Anonymous is a collective of people who acknowledge the defining idea as true.
Anonymous gained Media Attention in the early years of 2000 due to protests that involved hacking. Once again, Anonymous is NOT a 'hacktivist' collective, it is a collective that has resorted to hacking as a form of protest in the past because internet packets can't get shot with bullets, and it is easier to avoid jail.

2) How do I join Anonymous, Do I have to break the law?
A) By accepting the defining idea as truth (found on homepage), you become Anonymous.
Unless you live in an extremely tyrancial country such as North Korea, you do not need to break any laws. One of the things about being Anonymous is to speak freely. Free Speech is illegal in North Korea. We believe that laws that are morally wrong deserve to be broken, only good has the right of way in our eyes. Although some Anons decide to break the law in an act of deviant protest, it's not required to 'be anonymous'.
You can help Anonymous by sharing the idea, and helping others gain access to things that assist them in living freely, such as books, or showing them how to use Tor.

3) Do I need to be a hacker to be Anonymous?
A) No, but we recommend when you learn the basics about connecting to IRC or Tor.
Having access to Tor not only teaches you some basics, it gives you access to bypass censorship. If your media is corrupt, bought, or spreading mis-information, you will need the ability to bypass censorship to gain an awareness.
